Writing the report

A “report” can mean all sorts of things: It could refer to a published paper (very strict formatting) or more commonly a report to a supervisor or working group. Here, I will focus on creating a professional report for a normal workplace, which means a short paper with figures and nice formatting which explains your ideas and your work.

Traditional reports rely on a fairly strict structure:

  • Abstract - Very important section which summarizes the entire paper (see here for more)
  • Theory - Overview of which concepts and equations you are using
  • Experimental methods -
  • Analysis/Results
  • Conclusion
  • References

These sections are very nice to think about when writing a report, but explicitly writing a section for each of these categories is usually overkill for an internal report.
