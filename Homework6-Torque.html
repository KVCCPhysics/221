Homework 6 - Torque

Problem 6.1

In a manufacturing process, a large, cylindrical roller is used to flatten material fed beneath it. The diameter of the roller is 1.00 m, and, while being driven into rotation around a fixed axis, its angular position is expressed as \[\theta = 2.50 t^2 - 0.600t^3\] where \(\theta\) is in radians and \(t\) is in seconds.

  1. Find the maximum angular speed of the roller.
  2. What is the maximum tangential speed of a point on the rim of the roller?
  3. At what time t should the driving force be removed from the roller so that the roller does not reverse its direction of rotation?
  4. Through how many rotations has the roller turned between \(t = 0\) and the time found in part (c)?

Problem 6.3

A grinding wheel is in the form of a uniform solid disk of radius \(7.00~cm\) and mass \(2.00~kg\). It starts from rest and accelerates uniformly under the action of the constant torque of \(0.600~N\cdot m\) that the motor exerts on the wheel.

  1. How long does the wheel take to reach its final operating speed of \(1200~rev/min\)?
  2. Through how many revolutions does it turn while accelerating?

Problem 6.2

  1. the moment of inertia of the wheel
  2. the magnitude of the torque due to friction
  3. the total number of revolutions of the wheel during the entire interval of \(66.0~s\).
