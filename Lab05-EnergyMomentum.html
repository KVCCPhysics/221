3. Check your results

Lets look at the equation for the conservation of energy

\[W_{nc} = \Delta K + \Delta U\]

Recall that the non-conservative work (\(W_{NC}\)) has 3 choices:

  • \(W_{nc}>0\): Energy is being input from an external source (pushing the block, combusting gasoline, etc)
  • \(W_{nc}<0\): Energy is being expelled to an external object (sound, heat, matter transfer, EM radiation, etc)
  • \(W_{nc}=0\): Energy is unchanged (conserved) which was our assumption

4.1. Calculation of the error formula by hand

In order to perform the error calculations, we first need to write down the formula for the calculation of $Vball. Please take your time deriving this equation:

\[v_{ball}(m_1,m_2,g,\ell,\theta) = \frac{m_1+m_2}{m_1}\sqrt{2 g \ell \left(1-\cos \theta \right)}\]

Some of these measurements are much more accurate than the rest. For example the mass calculation is very accurate (error of \(0.0001~kg\) with a load balance scale) and the gravitational acceleration is \(g=9.80284~m/s^2\) in Kalamazoo. Because these values have high precision and accuracy, lets exclude them from the error calculation. The function then becomes:

\[v_{ball}(\ell,\theta) = \frac{m_1+m_2}{m_1}\sqrt{2 g \ell \left(1-\cos \theta \right)}\]

The error calculations now require we calculate (remember \(\sigma[\ell]\) and \(\sigma[\theta]\) are constants, they are equal to the standard deviations calculated earlier): \[\sigma[v_{ball}] = \sqrt{\left( \frac{\partial v_{ball}}{\partial \ell}\cdot \sigma[\ell]\right)^2 + \left( \frac{\partial v_{ball}}{\partial \theta}\cdot \sigma[\theta]\right)^2}\]

# Function to perform error propogation on (must be symbolic)
eqn = (m1+m2)/m1*sympy.sqrt(g*l*(2-2*sympy.cos(theta)))

# list of measured values (note: double ==)
measure=[(l,0.29),
         (El,0.01),

#The error formula: E[eqn]

            (sympy.diff(eqn,l) * El)**2 +
            (sympy.diff(eqn,theta) * Etheta)**2

# display the equation and its error

# substitute measurements into eqn and error functions
print("velocity =", eqn.subs(measure),"+-",error.subs(measure),"m/s")
