Vectors and Scalars

There are two different types of variables used in this physics class, scalars and vectors:

Scalar: a scalar is a normal variable like you have used in your math classes. the value of this variable is known as the magnitude. Some examples of scalar quantities are mass, time, temperature, and moment of inertia.

Vector: A vector quantity also has a value (referred to as its magnitude), but now includes added information: the direction. There are many vector quantities that you will study in this class. Examples are position velocity acceleration force momentum etc.

Fortunately, you do not have to memorize which type of variable is a vector and which is a scalar. You can simply think about the variable and figure out if it is a vector or a scalar. I will illustrate this with two examples:

Example 1: Mass

When defining the mass of an object (let’s say your laptop computer), you can completely define the quantity by simply stating the number of kilograms of mass the object has. It does not make sense to say that the computer has a mass of m=0.5kg in the upward direction. The lack of directional information means that this quantity is a scalar.

Example 2: Position

Imagine you are trying to describe your movements around your house. If you move from your desk to the bathroom, you will need to give information on which way to move. You cannot simply say “walk a distance of 4.6m and you will arrive in the bathroom”. You may walk the incorrect direction and end up in the kitchen instead. The requirement of this directional information means that this quantity is a vector.

There are two ways to describe vectors, one is graphical the other is mathematical. Both of these abstractions are useful when trying to find a solution to a vector physics problem, so you will have to pay attention to both of them.

Graphical: The visual way to describe a vector uses an arrow. Arrows are convenient because they include both direction and magnitude:

Direction: the way that the arrow is pointing Magnitude: the length of the arrow

Mathematical: A picture is very useful for helping get your head around a problem, but it is not useful when actually solving for unknowns in a problem. Instead, you will have to rely on mathematics, which uses “ordered pairs” to describe the vector. As an example, the vector for the position would be written like this: LaTeX: \vec{x} = ( x, y)x → = ( x , y )
